Special Characters

Special characters (also called meta-characters) are a group of characters that bash reads as special instuctions that it will carry out, some special characters are the ones that follow.

Whitespace

Whitespace is a tab, newline, vertical tab, form feed, carriage return, or space. Bash will use whitespace as a way to tell when words start and finish.

Expansion

There are multiple kinds of expansion, including parameter expansion, command substitution and arithmitic expansion.

Comment

Comment leaves words that you type that is left alone and not read as a command.

Double Quotes

Double comments group words together so they arent read as single words or arguments.
